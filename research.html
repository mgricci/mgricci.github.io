Research
I study machine learning methods for optimally controlling neural dynamics, particularly oscillatory dynamics. I am interested how these methods can be used to understand the cortical processes underlying higher cognition .

Current projects address

  • Optimal control of the Kuramoto model for phase-based data clustering
  • Kuramoto phase-locking in complex networks
  • Glassy behavior in oscillator networks
  • Variational methods for generating adversarial distributions. This is joint work with Drew Linsley .
